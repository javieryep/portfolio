SAP CX Live — crafting B2B2C prototypes

Role

  • Product designer
  • Based in Palo Alto, California, working remotely with the SAP Customer Experience Labs, an innovation team based in Waldorf, Germany
  • Fellowship duration: 3 months

What is SAP CX Live?

It is SAP’s annual customer experience conference, which takes place in Orlando, Florida. It targets SAP customers who are interested in current and future technologies SAP is building to improve customer experience. It is the main stage to showcase future partnerships, exciting new technology and ideas.

Project goal

One of the highlights for the conference was the SAP Cloud Platform Extension Factory, also known as Kyma, which allows to extend applications with serverless functions and microservices. Kyma enables startups to integrate their systems with the larger SAP ecosystem much more easily. The goal of my 3-month fellowship with SAP CX Labs was to help create prototype demos in order

to highlight Kyma by showcasing startup products integrated with the SAP Cloud Platform.

I worked with 2 startups in their product presentations—Thinfilm and Brickwork Software—under the guidance of David Brockington, the Head of Innovation Ecosystem at SAP CX Labs.

1st project

Thinfilm—‘tap to’ consumer experience

Thinfilm is a San Jose-based company, whose value proposition is to enhance the customer experience with non-electronic items by adding unique near field communication tags, or NFC tags, providing both the customer and the seller with valuable contextual actionable information. In spite of this, their initial presentation proposal used kitchen appliances, which seemed to downplay the presentation impact because we assumed users would already expect these appliances to have a level of connectivity.

In order to give Thinfilm’s presentation the surprise effect we wanted, we brainstormed to find an item that would replace these appliances by fulfilling these requirements:

  1. does not have any electronic parts and internet connectivity,
  2. consumers are emotionally related to,
  3. and are of everyday use.

After a series of ideas, we came up with a fictional protein powder brand called MuscleUp, around which we tailored the narrative.

We defined the user journey based on current and future-facing capabilities of Thinfilm’s NFC technology. The whole experience would happen on a mobile device, and the prototype would account for multiple use cases.

Scenario Customer need Proposed solution
In-store product search The customer is at the store considering buying the product and needs to receive accurate product information. Marketing content (videos, reviews, testimonials, etc.)
Purchase consideration The customer needs to know how this product compares to other product options. Product comparison table, populated by tapping products
Product regular usage The customer is probably at home and needs contextual information on how to use the product. Geolocation, account creation, marketing content (recipes, exercise tips, etc.)
Product is about to expire The user needs to be notified on time and have the option to reorder the product. Notifications, in-app ecommerce
Wrong shipment The user received the wrong item and needs to access customer support and personalized assistance. In-app live chat

With a redefined storyline and use cases in place, I proceeded to create the prototype wireframes, which later evolved into the high fidelity screens.

The presenter of this product demo was David Brockington himself, who used physical protein powder jars on stage to demonstrate the product vision. Since then, Thinfilm has changed its value proposition to high-capacity and ultra-thin lithium batteries.

2nd project

Brickwork—blending online and in-store retail

Brickwork Software is a New-York based company that provides a unified checkout experience to customers purchasing both physical items and in-person experiences offered on the same e-commerce site. Examples of these in-person experiences include NikeLab training session, fitting appointments at Kate Spade, Bonobo’s personal shopping experiences, Bassett’s design consultations, etc.

During the development of this presentation, the Chief Innovation Officer of SAP Customer Experience Eugenio Cassiano and Brickwork’s founder and CEO David Munczinski were personally involved and who I met with a few times.

After this presentation took place, Brickwork Software was acquired by b8ta, a San Francisco-based retail company.

Results

Exposure to 4000+ SAP customers during the 3-day conference

Some key learnings

  • Working remotely requires extra effort to communicate clearly. I learned to setting intentional time to explain my thought process.
  • Having a dedicated front-end developer helped me learn alot about how to develop responsive web applications more efficiently.
  • As part of a fast-paced young startup, I had to get creative to research and test my designs by reaching out to those whose profile would fit our manager persona for direct feedback.
  • As the only designer in the team, I became the spokesperson of the product and brand inside SAP and our external users.
