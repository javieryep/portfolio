About

As a product designer, I've been working for Silicon Valley enterprise software companies with agile methodologies for the last few years. At my core, I strive to transform complex processes into intuitive and seamless experiences.

I believe good experience design is an equalizer of success for people who are technologically underserved, especially in complex tasks.

In my daily work, I collaborate with researchers, project managers, engineers and copywriters to create value for customers. My design decisions are often marked by the less is more approach, but only as long as clarity and accessibility are not compromised.

My journey has had multiple turns

I come from a humble home in Peru, where I grew up fascinated by arts, technology and philosophy. I majored in engineering for three years, but then I felt compelled to follow my faith more closely in a Catholic seminary. As my experience deepened, I discovered I was called to serve God in the secular life.

This was also my chance to pursue a carreer in design, which I found to be the intersection of my passions. I moved to Costa Rica to study advertising design, where I also worked as an anthropology college professor and a freelance brand designer for some years.

My fascination for Silicon Valley pushed me to immigrate to the United States. I completed my design degree at SJSU and worked in multiple teams at SAP as a product designer in the San Francisco Bay Area. I later moved eastward to work for DocuSign in downtown Chicago in the automation of agreement generation and negotiation, at the SMB and enterprise market levels.

What I'm up to

Like many others, I'm currently quarantining myself at home and working remotely. If I'm not drawing, you might find me teaching myself a new creative skill, reading, or most likely playing basketball.
